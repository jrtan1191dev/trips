### 🛍️ General Shopping & Malls (Top 30 Spots)

*Malls are the ultimate family travel hack in Japan. They offer immaculate, free baby care rooms (nursing rooms, hot water for formula, changing tables), wide aisles, elevators, and incredible food halls in the basement.*

1. **Tokyo Skytree & Solamachi** (Oshiage) - Huge mall beneath the tower, easy train access.
2. **Sunshine City** (Ikebukuro) - Massive indoor complex with an aquarium and Pokémon center.
3. **DiverCity Tokyo Plaza** (Odaiba) - Home of the giant Gundam statue.
4. **Urban Dock LaLaport** (Toyosu) - Elite family mall overlooking the ocean.
5. **GINZA SIX** (Ginza) - Ultra-luxury mall, world-class rooftop garden, amazing baby facilities.
6. **Shibuya Scramble Square** (Shibuya) - Directly above Shibuya station, elite food hall.
7. **Miyashita Park** (Shibuya) - Open-air mall with a public park on the roof.
8. **Roppongi Hills** (Roppongi) - Elite corporate/shopping hub with premium family facilities.
9. **Tokyo Midtown** (Roppongi) - Upscale mall attached to a sprawling, gorgeous park.
10. **KITTE** (Marunouchi) - Beautiful mall next to Tokyo Station.
11. **Shinjuku Takashimaya Times Square** (Shinjuku) - Fantastic high-end department store.
12. **Isetan Shinjuku** (Shinjuku) - Famous for having the best basement food hall (Depachika) in Japan.
13. **Omotesando Hills** (Harajuku) - Designed with a continuous ramp, meaning zero elevator waiting!
14. **Aqua City** (Odaiba) - Massive mall right on the water.
15. **Decks Tokyo Beach** (Odaiba) - Home to indoor theme parks.
16. **Tokyu Plaza Omotesando** (Harajuku) - Famous mirrored entrance, Starbucks rooftop garden.
17. **Yaesu Underground Mall** (Tokyo Station) - Incredible for rainy days.
18. **Ginza Mitsukoshi** (Ginza) - Traditional high-end department store right on the crossing.
19. **Matsuya Ginza** (Ginza) - Excellent basement food hall.
20. **COREDO Muromachi** (Nihonbashi) - Beautiful, modern take on traditional Japanese architecture.
21. **Shibuya Parco** (Shibuya) - The "geek" mall (Nintendo Tokyo, Pokémon Center, Capcom).
22. **Ariake Garden** (Odaiba area) - Massive new mall built specifically with families in mind.
23. **Grandberry Park** (Machida - Day trip) - Premium outlet mall attached to a massive park.
24. **Yodobashi Camera Multimedia Akiba** (Akihabara) - 9 floors of electronics, toys, and gacha machines.
25. **Don Quijote Asakusa** (Asakusa) - Wider aisles than the Shinjuku/Shibuya branches.
26. **Akachan Honpo Gotanda TOC** (Gotanda) - One of the largest baby stores in Tokyo.
27. **Akachan Honpo Kinshicho** (Kinshicho) - Huge branch, 10 mins from Skytree.
28. **Toys"R"Us / Babies"R"Us** (Odaiba) - Massive selection of localized Japanese baby goods.

### 🎡 Sightseeing Attractions & Scenery

*With a stroller and 6 adults, outdoor spaces and major aquariums/museums are your best bet. Avoid tiny historical alleyways (like Omoide Yokocho/Golden Gai) which cannot fit strollers.*

#### 🌸 Top Sakura (Cherry Blossom) Viewing Spots

* **Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden** (Shinjuku) - Massive, pristine park. Perfect for strollers and one of Tokyo's most famous sakura spots. [Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Shinjuku%20Gyoen%20National%20Garden%20Tokyo)
* **Yoyogi Park** (Harajuku) - Huge open park, extremely family friendly with wide paths and many cherry trees. [Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Yoyogi%20Park%20Tokyo)
* **Meguro River** (Nakameguro) - Beautiful tree-lined walking paths, arguably Tokyo's most famous and aesthetic cherry blossom night illumination spot. [Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Meguro%20River%20Tokyo)
* **Hana Biyori** (Inagi) - Entertainment botanical garden next to Yomiuriland with indoor flower chandeliers, Starbucks, and digital art mapping. Very unique sakura viewing! [Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hana%20Biyori%20Tokyo)
* **Matsudayama Herb Garden / Nishihirabatake Park** (Matsuda - Day Trip) - Famous for its Matsuda Cherry Blossom Festival featuring stunning early-blooming Kawazu Sakura on the mountain slope with Mt. Fuji views! [Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Nishihirabatake%20Park%20Matsuda)
* **Ueno Park** (Ueno) - One of Japan's most crowded, famous, and lively spots for cherry blossom viewing parties (hanami). Features a massive central pathway lined with over 1,000 cherry trees! [Map Location](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Ueno%20Park%20Tokyo)
