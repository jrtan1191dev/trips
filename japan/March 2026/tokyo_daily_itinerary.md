# 🌸 Tokyo 7-Day "Perfect Spread" Itinerary (Mar 18 - Mar 24, 2026)

**Group Size:** 6 Adults + 1 Infant (Stroller)
**Pacing:** Geographically optimized to minimize transit; food categories strictly rotated to guarantee maximum variety without overlapping.

---

## 🛬 Day 1: Arrival & Shinjuku Landing (Wed, Mar 18)

**Cuisine Focus:** Cafe Food / Omurice & Premium Hotpot
**Shopping Focus:** Late-night essentials
**Scenery Focus:** Early Sakura (Cherry Blossoms)

* **08:40 AM - Land at NRT:** Clear customs, pick up Pocket Wi-Fi/activate eSims.
* **09:30 AM - Airport Transfer:** Pre-booked Klook transfer directly from NRT to Hotel Groove Shinjuku.
* **11:00 AM - Drop Bags:** Arrive at **Hotel Groove Shinjuku** in Kabukicho. Drop luggage at the front desk.
* **11:30 AM - Transit:** Take a quick train ride (Yamanote or Fukutoshin Line) down to Shibuya.
* **12:00 PM - Lunch [Omurice / Cafe]:** Eat at **EDW Yellow Shibuya**. A highly popular cafe famous for its fluffy omurice and pancakes. 
* **02:00 PM - Scenery:** Take the Fukutoshin subway line directly back to Shinjuku-sanchome station. Walk to **Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden**. It is massive, perfectly flat for strollers, and one of the absolute best spots for early/mid-March Sakura.
* **04:30 PM - Hotel Check-in:** Return to Hotel Groove to freshen up.
* **06:30 PM - Dinner [Sukiyaki / Shabu Shabu]:** **Nabezo Shinjuku Sanchome**. Fantastic, spacious booth seating for the group to enjoy high-quality all-you-can-eat Japanese hotpot.
* **08:30 PM - Shopping [Essentials]:** **Don Quijote (Kabukicho Store)**. Very close to your hotel. Visit now before the late-night crowds hit.
* **10:30 PM - Supper [Ramen]:** **Ichiran Kabukicho**. *Note: 6 adults cannot sit together here. Split into groups of 3 and take turns while the other group watches the infant at the hotel.*

## 👗 Day 2: The Western Hub - Shibuya & Harajuku (Thu, Mar 19)

**Cuisine Focus:** Okonomiyaki & Conveyor Belt Sushi
**Dessert Focus:** Viral Pastries & Coffee
**Scenery Focus:** Yoyogi Park & Iconic Tokyo Crossings

* **09:30 AM - Scenery (Sakura):** Take the Yamanote line to Harajuku Station. Explore the massive, family-friendly **Yoyogi Park**, enjoying one of Tokyo's best-known natural escapes and its lovely cherry blossoms.
* **11:30 AM - Dessert [Viral Pastry]:** Grab the viral **GARIGUETTE** (Mille-presse) as takeout in Omotesando and eat it while pushing the stroller down the avenue.
* **12:30 PM - Lunch [Okonomiyaki]:** **Sakura-tei (Harajuku)**. A massive, artistic, and loud warehouse where the group can comfortably cook savory pancakes together.
* **02:30 PM - Shopping [Fashion/Ramps]:** **Omotesando Hills**. The entire interior of this mall is a continuous, gentle ramp—you never have to wait for an elevator with the stroller!
* **04:30 PM - Scenery:** Head to Shibuya. Take the classic group photo at **Shibuya Crossing** and visit the **Hachiko** statue.
* **06:30 PM - Dinner [Kaiten Sushi]:** **Katsumidori Seibu Shibuya**. A premium conveyor belt sushi spot located inside the Seibu department store. Great booths, easy touchscreen ordering.
* **08:30 PM - Shopping [Trendy / General]:** Explore **Shibuya Scramble Square** directly over the station, or the trendy, open-air structure of **Miyashita Park** for general fashion, lifestyle goods, and beautiful rooftop views.

## 🏙️ Day 3: Hotel Transfer & Ginza Luxury (Fri, Mar 20)

**Cuisine Focus:** Historic Tempura & High-end Dining
**Dessert Focus:** Premium Matcha Parfaits
**Scenery Focus:** Imperial Palace & Luxury Architecture

* **09:00 AM - Check Out & Logistics:** Check out of Hotel Groove. *Ship your 6 large suitcases to the Asakusa hotel via Yamato Transport (Ta-Q-Bin)* at the front desk. Travel today with just the stroller and a day bag!
* **10:30 AM - Scenery:** Explore the **Imperial Palace East Gardens** or the **KITTE** mall rooftop garden next to Tokyo Station.
* **12:30 PM - Lunch [Tempura]:** **Tenichi Ginza Hon Ten**. Historic tempura shop that explicitly welcomes children and has private rooms available.
* **02:30 PM - Shopping [Luxury]:** Head to **GINZA SIX**. Ultra-luxury mall with a world-class rooftop garden (perfect for letting the baby crawl) and elite baby care facilities.
* **03:30 PM - Dessert [Premium Matcha]:** **Nakamura Tokichi Honten** (inside Ginza SIX). Skip the coffee today and indulge in top-tier Kyoto matcha parfaits in a spacious environment.
* **05:30 PM - PRE-BOOKED DINNER:** **Ginza Happo**. Enjoy your secured reservation!
* **08:00 PM - Check-in:** Head to Asakusa and check into **Far East Village Hotel Tokyo, Asakusa**. Your forwarded luggage should arrive tonight or tomorrow morning.

## 🏝️ Day 4: Odaiba/Ariake (Sat, Mar 21)

**Cuisine Focus:** Yakiniku (Japanese BBQ)
**Shopping Focus:** Leisurely Waterfront Mall
**Scenery Focus:** Miniature Worlds & Ocean Views

* **09:30 AM - Transit to Odaiba:** Take a scenic train ride or the Tokyo Cruise water bus from Asakusa down to the Odaiba/Ariake area.
* **10:30 AM - Scenery (Miniature Park):** **Small Worlds Tokyo (Ariake)**. An intricate, sprawling indoor miniature theme park. It is flat, climate-controlled, very stroller-friendly, and perfect for families.
* **01:00 PM - Lunch [Casual/Food Court]:** The food court at **Urban Dock LaLaport Toyosu** is massive, loud, and perfect for a group of 6. Grab ramen, takoyaki, or udon while enjoying views of the ocean.
* **02:30 PM - Shopping / Relaxation:** Stroll around the LaLaport Toyosu complex. It is known as an elite family mall with wide open outdoor decks next to the water, perfect for letting the baby out of the stroller.
* **05:00 PM - Return to Asakusa:** Head back to your hotel area to relax before dinner.
* **06:30 PM - Dinner [Yakiniku]:** **Ore no Yakiniku Asakusa**. Book a private room to keep the baby safely away from the BBQ open flames while the group enjoys Japanese beef.

## 🏮 Day 5: Traditional Asakusa (Sun, Mar 22)

**Cuisine Focus:** Soba Noodles & (2nd) Conveyor Belt Sushi
**Dessert Focus:** Viral Pancakes & Intense Matcha Gelato
**Scenery Focus:** Historic Temples & Shrines

* **07:30 AM - Scenery:** Go early! The **Nakamise shopping street** and **Senso-ji Temple** get impossibly crowded by 10 AM, making strollers very difficult.
* **08:00 AM - Dessert [Viral Pancakes]:** While the group explores Senso-ji, send one adult to queue at **Benitsuru** (just blocks away) to secure a time-slot ticket for their famous fluffy pancakes for later in the morning/afternoon.
* **09:30 AM - Scenery:** Walk to **Imado Shrine** (the "Must Visit" beckoning cat shrine). It's a peaceful 15-minute walk from the main Asakusa area.
* **11:30 AM - Lunch [Soba]:** **Namiki Yabusoba**. Famous, historic soba shop. Fast turnover makes it easier for groups.
* **01:30 PM - Dessert [Richest Matcha Gelato]:** **Suzukien Asakusa**. Grab the world famous 7-tier matcha gelato. Expect a line, but the queue moves fast since there is no seating.
* **03:00 PM - Shopping [Souvenirs & Crafts]:** Explore the surrounding Asakusa streets or head back to the hotel for an infant nap.
* **06:00 PM - Dinner [(2nd) Kaiten Sushi]:** **Kura Sushi Asakusa ROX**. This is the global flagship store. It is massive, highly entertaining, and features huge booths perfect for your group.

## 🗼 Day 6: Ueno & Baby Haul (Mon, Mar 23)

**Cuisine Focus:** Izakaya (Gastropub)
**Dessert Focus:** Fruit Mille-Crepe Cakes
**Shopping Focus:** Mega Baby Haul (Akachan Honpo)
**Scenery Focus:** Animal Parks & Museums

* **09:30 AM - Scenery (Sakura):** Head to Ueno. Walk the massive central pathway of **Ueno Park**. It will be extremely crowded and lively with cherry blossom viewing parties, but the wide paths are fine for strollers.
* **11:00 AM - Scenery:** Visit either the **National Museum of Nature & Science** (world class dinosaur exhibit) or **Ueno Zoo** (perfect for toddlers/infants). Both are highly stroller accessible.
* **01:00 PM - Lunch [Casual Station Mall]:** Grab lunch in the Ueno Station complex (ecute Ueno) which has many spacious, accessible restaurants.
* **02:30 PM - Dessert [Fruit Cakes]:** Walk over to **HARBS** (inside PARCO_ya Ueno) and sit down for their legendary, massive fruit mille-crepe cakes. 
* **04:00 PM - Shopping [Baby Bulk Haul]:** Take a short train ride from Ueno directly to Kinshicho. Spend the late afternoon at **Akachan Honpo (Kinshicho)**. This is a massive flagship branch where you can buy bulk diapers, Japanese snacks, and unique baby merchandise to safely pack into your suitcases tonight before departure.
* **07:00 PM - Dinner [Izakaya/Yakitori]:** Kinshicho is an izakaya hub. Find a spacious, family-friendly Izakaya like **Torikizoku** nearby, or eat at the Kinshicho Termina station mall before heading back to the hotel.

## 🛫 Day 7: Relaxing Asakusa Morning & Departure (Tue, Mar 24)

**Cuisine Focus:** Unagi (Grilled Eel) & Asakusa Cafes
**Shopping Focus:** Last-Minute Souvenirs
**Logistics Focus:** Stress-free Airport Transit

* **09:00 AM - Check Out & Store Bags:** Check out of Far East Village Hotel but have them hold your luggage at the front desk.
* **09:30 AM - Scenery & Coffee:** Take a short, relaxing stroll from your hotel to the **Sumida River Walk** and **Tokyo Mizumachi** (the trendy pathway along the river). Grab coffee and pastries from one of the waterfront cafes.
* **11:00 AM - Last-Minute Shopping:** Stroll back through the quieter streets of Asakusa near your hotel to grab any final Japanese snacks or souvenirs.
* **12:00 PM - Final Lunch [Unagi!]:** Treat yourselves to a traditional Unagi (Eel) rice box feast to end the trip! **Unatoto Asakusa** is extremely close by, famous for delivering fantastic, fast, and highly affordable grilled eel rice bowls. 
* **01:00 PM - Luggage Collection:** Return to Far East Village Hotel Asakusa to collect all 6 suitcases and the stroller.
* **01:30 PM - Airport Transfer:** Pre-booked Klook transfer picks you up directly from Far East Village Hotel Asakusa and drives you to NRT.
* **03:00 PM - Arrive at NRT:** Arrive at Narita Airport for your 5:05 PM ANA (Air Japan) flight NQ003. Check-in, grab any last-minute duty-free items, and have a safe flight home!
