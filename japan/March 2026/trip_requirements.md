## Transportation & Logistics (Crucial for Large Groups & Infant)

* **Airport Transfers:**
  * **NRT to Shinjuku:** Recommend Airport Limousine Bus (direct to major Shinjuku hotels) or booking a Private Jumbo Taxi/Chartered Van for the group + stroller + luggage. Avoid the commuter trains.
  * **Asakusa to NRT:** Keisei Skyliner from Keisei Ueno Station is the fastest route back.
* **Moving Between Hotels:** Use **Yamato Transport (Ta-Q-Bin)** to forward your large suitcases from Shinjuku to Asakusa. Pack an overnight bag for the transfer day. This avoids dragging 6+ suitcases and a stroller through crowded subways.
* **Station Accessibility:** Factor in extra travel time to find elevators (often at the far ends of platforms and require multiple transfers).
* **Connectivity:** Ensure everyone has their own eSIM (like Ubigi or Airalo) instead of sharing one Pocket Wi-Fi, as the group of 6 will inevitably need to split up at times.

## Must-Visit

* **Akachan Honpo:** A one-stop shop for baby merchandise. (Branches in TOC Gotanda, or Kinshicho Kitaguchi are very large).
* **Imado Shrine:** A shrine famous for maneki-neko (beckoning cats), located in Asakusa.
* **Sakura viewing (Less crowded options):**
  * **Shinjuku Gyoen:** Huge park near your first hotel. Very family/stroller-friendly. (May require advance reservations during peak bloom).
  * **Yanaka Cemetery:** Near Asakusa/Ueno. A beautiful, quieter, and more respectful atmosphere compared to Ueno Park.
  * **Kiba Park / Sarue Onshi Park:** Further east, spacious and mostly frequented by locals.
* **Don Quijote (Based on hotels):**
  * **Shinjuku Kabukicho Store:** Very close to Hotel Groove. *Tip: Kabukicho gets very crowded/rowdy at night; visit early morning with the infant.*
  * **Asakusa Store:** Short walk from your second hotel. Generally easier to navigate with a family than Shinjuku branches.

## Dining & Supper Preferences

* **Ichiran Ramen (Based on hotels):**
  * **Shinjuku Kabukicho Store:** Located right in Kabukicho, extremely close to Hotel Groove.
  * **Asakusa Store:** Located a short walk from Far East Village Hotel Tokyo, Asakusa.
  * *Note on Logistics:* Ichiran uses individual flavor-concentration booths. It is almost impossible to seat 6 people together, and there is no space for a stroller. You will likely need to split into groups of 2-3, or take turns watching the infant outside.
* **Large Group Dining Alternatives:** Look for restaurants offering *Koshitsu* (private rooms) or family restaurants (*Famiresu* like Royal Host, Gusto, Denny's) which have spacious booth seating suitable for infants.

## Infant Care & Emergencies

* **Nursing/Changing Rooms:** Utilize large department stores (Isetan or Takashimaya in Shinjuku, Matsuya in Asakusa). They have immaculate, fully-equipped baby care rooms with hot water, changing tables, and private nursing areas.
* **Emergency Numbers:** 119 for Ambulance/Fire. 110 for Police.
